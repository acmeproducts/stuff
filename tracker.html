Welcome to the Tracking Demonstration App

Explore each tab to learn more about how websites can track your data.

IP Address Tracking

IP addresses are unique identifiers for devices on the internet. They can reveal your geographic location and internet service provider.

Allow IP Address Tracking

DNS Requests

DNS requests can be monitored to see which websites and domains you are accessing, providing a history of visited sites.

Allow DNS Request Tracking

HTTP Headers

HTTP headers include information such as user-agent strings, which reveal details about your browser and operating system, as well as referrer information.

Allow HTTP Header Tracking

Device Fingerprinting

Device fingerprinting collects a set of attributes that are unique to your device, such as screen resolution, installed fonts, and time zone settings.

Allow Device Fingerprinting

Browser Fingerprinting

Browser fingerprinting uses a combination of browser attributes, such as installed plugins, screen resolution, and browser settings, to create a unique identifier for you.

Allow Browser Fingerprinting

Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that can track your preferences and behavior across websites. They can be first-party or third-party.

Allow Cookie Tracking

Local Storage and Session Storage

Web storage solutions allow websites to store larger amounts of data on your device, which can be used to track your behavior and preferences.
